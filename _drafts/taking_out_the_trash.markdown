Ours is a life in transition. We are renting a townhome in an apartment community in a new state. It's a nice enough place, but everything about it feels temporary, especially the people. Nobody here is particularly interested in getting to know one another. There are a few fellow relocators, but most are here either to start a family or because their family has fallen apart.

When we moved in, I checked the mail to see if anything had been forwarded from our previous address. The mailbox held nothing for me, but there were a couple of letters for the previous tenant. Both were from Child Protective Services. We received about ten more such letters over the next few weeks. After about a month, we received a notice on our door from an agency that locates people. The notice said that they wanted to ask some questions "about a neighbor." I called. A pleasant woman asked my case number, which I gave her.

"Do you live at 27 Deadbeat Drive?"

"No, I live at 29."

An awkward silence followed.

"Oh, um, sorry for the inconvenience. You can disregard the notice."

I haven't received any letters from CPS since. I tell myself they found him.

I work from home, so I have learned a bit about my neighbors. There's a man across the street who has a taste for sports cars. He had a Tesla delivered shortly after we moved. I didn't even know you could have a car delivered to your house. I'd like to try that out, but I don't think I'm buying the right cars.

I've never spoken to Tesla. A couple of weeks ago, I was taking out the trash and saw him standing in his driveway. I decided to take the long way around so I could walk past and say hello. I tried to catch his eye as I approached, but couldn't. He was on his phone. As I passed, I heard him say, "I feel like I have to spend everything now so she can't go after it in court."

There is a communal trash compactor a couple of blocks from our unit. I like to walk to it - it's a reminder of the more urban life we left when we moved here. It can be reached from a set of stairs in the front or an inclined path that wraps around the back. As I approached it after my encounter with Tesla, I saw a huge pile of trash blocking the steps. The pile of trash was so large that I had to walk around the compactor to get to it.
